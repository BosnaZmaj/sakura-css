How to create your first budget

Learn the fundamentals of envelope budgeting and set up your first budget in under 5 minutes. This guide will walk you through every step to get you started on your financial journey.

Last updated: January 15, 2025

Understanding envelope budgeting

Envelope budgeting is a simple yet powerful method where you divide your income into different spending categories, just like putting cash into physical envelopes. Each envelope represents a specific purpose for your money.

Why it works:

When you can physically see how much money is left in each category, you make better spending decisions. Digital envelopes in Sakura Budget work the same way.

The core principle is straightforward: when an envelope is empty, you stop spending in that category until you refill it next month. This prevents overspending and helps you prioritize what matters most.

Setting up your income

Before you can allocate money, you need to tell Sakura Budget how much income you have to work with each month.

  1. Navigate to the Dashboard

    From your main dashboard, click the "Add Income" button in the top right corner.

  2. Enter your monthly income

    Include all sources: salary, freelance work, side hustles, etc. If your income varies, use an average or conservative estimate.

  3. Set the frequency

    Choose whether you're paid weekly, bi-weekly, or monthly. Sakura Budget will automatically calculate your monthly total.

Pro Tip:

If your income varies month to month, start with last month's income. This ensures you're budgeting money you actually have, not money you hope to earn.

Creating your first envelopes

Now for the fun part - creating envelopes for your spending categories. Start with the essentials and add more as you get comfortable.

Essential envelopes to start with:

  • Housing - Rent or mortgage, utilities, internet
  • Food - Groceries and dining out
  • Transportation - Car payment, gas, insurance, public transit
  • Savings - Emergency fund and other savings goals
  • Personal - Clothes, entertainment, hobbies

Screenshot: Creating a new envelope

How to create an envelope:

  1. Click the "New Envelope" button on your dashboard
  2. Choose a descriptive name (e.g., "Groceries" instead of just "Food")
  3. Pick a color to help you identify it quickly
  4. Set a spending limit based on your typical expenses
  5. Click "Create Envelope"

Allocating your money

With your income entered and envelopes created, it's time to allocate money to each envelope. This is where your budget comes to life.

Important:

Your total allocated amount should not exceed your income. Sakura Budget will show you how much money remains unallocated as you go.

The 50/30/20 rule as a starting point:

If you're not sure how to divide your income, try this popular budgeting framework:

  • 50% - Needs - Housing, food, transportation, utilities
  • 30% - Wants - Entertainment, dining out, hobbies
  • 20% - Savings - Emergency fund, retirement, goals

For example, if you earn $3,000 per month:

  • $1,500 for needs (housing, food, transportation)
  • $900 for wants (fun stuff)
  • $600 for savings

Tracking your spending

Once your budget is set up, the key to success is consistently tracking where your money goes.

Automatic tracking with bank sync:

The easiest way to track spending is by connecting your bank accounts. Sakura Budget will:

  • Import transactions automatically
  • Categorize them into your envelopes
  • Update your envelope balances in real-time
Success Habit:

Check your budget every few days. Regular check-ins help you stay aware of your spending and make adjustments before problems arise.

Manual entry:

Prefer to enter transactions yourself? That works too:

  1. Click "Add Transaction" from any envelope
  2. Enter the amount and description
  3. Assign it to the appropriate envelope
  4. Save the transaction

Pro tips for success

Start simple

Begin with 5-7 envelopes. You can always add more later as you understand your spending patterns better.

Be realistic

Look at your last month's spending to set realistic limits. Overly restrictive budgets often fail within weeks.

Include fun money

Budget for entertainment and personal spending. A budget isn't about deprivation - it's about intentional spending.

Review monthly

At the end of each month, review what worked and what didn't. Adjust your envelopes for the next month accordingly.

Don't give up

The first month is always the hardest. Stick with it for 3 months before deciding if envelope budgeting works for you.

Celebrate wins

Acknowledge when you stay under budget or reach a savings goal. Small wins build lasting habits.

What's next?

Congratulations! You've created your first budget. Here are some next steps to explore:

  • Connect your bank account for automatic transaction tracking
  • Set up your first savings goal to stay motivated
  • Learn about envelope categories to organize your budget better
  • Explore spending reports to understand your habits
