Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 10, 2025

1. Introduction

At Sakura Financial Technologies LLC ("Sakura," "we," "us," or "our"), we take your privacy seriously. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, share, and protect your personal information when you use Sakura Budget, our web-based envelope budgeting platform (the "Services").

This Privacy Policy applies to all users of our Services and is incorporated into our Terms of Service. By using Sakura Budget, you agree to the collection, use, and sharing of your information as described in this Privacy Policy.

Important: We do NOT sell your financial data or budget information. Your financial privacy is fundamental to our business model, which is based on subscriptions, not advertising or data sales.

2. Information We Collect

We collect several types of information to provide and improve our Services:

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Account Information: Email address, password (encrypted), and account preferences
  • Financial Data (Manual): Budget categories you create, envelope allocations, spending goals, and transactions you manually enter
  • Communication Data: Support requests, feedback, and correspondence with us
  • Payment Information: Billing details for subscription payments (processed securely through third-party payment processors)

Note: Automated financial data collected from your bank accounts is covered separately in Section 2.2 below.

2.2 Financial Account Information via Third-Party Services

When you connect your bank accounts using our bank connection feature, we use third-party financial data aggregation services to securely retrieve:

  • Account Information: Account names, types, balances, and account numbers (last 4 digits)
  • Transaction Data (Automated): Transaction descriptions, amounts, dates, merchants, and categories from your connected financial institutions
  • Identity Information: Account holder names and basic account details

Important Security Note: We NEVER see or store your bank login credentials (username and password). These credentials are handled exclusively by our financial data provider using bank-level encryption. Sakura cannot access your bank login information at any time.

By connecting your financial accounts, you agree to the privacy practices of our financial data providers. You can view the current provider's privacy policy and manage access through your account settings or their dedicated portals.

2.3 Automatically Collected Information

  • Usage Data: Features used, pages viewed, time spent on the platform, and interaction patterns
  • Device Information: Device type, operating system, browser type and version, IP address, and unique device identifiers
  • Log Data: Access times, error logs, and system activity
  • Cookies and Tracking: Session cookies, preference cookies, and analytics cookies (see Section 11 for details)

2.4 Information from Third Parties

  • Analytics Providers: Aggregated usage statistics and performance metrics
  • Security Services: Fraud prevention and security monitoring data
  • Service Providers: Data necessary to operate our Services (email delivery, cloud hosting)

3. How We Use Your Information

We use your information for the following specific purposes:

3.1 To Provide Our Services

  • Create and manage your account
  • Display your budgets, envelopes, and financial data
  • Connect to your financial institutions through third-party financial data services
  • Categorize and track your transactions (both manual and automated)
  • Calculate budget allocations and financial goals
  • Provide spending insights and reports

3.2 To Improve and Develop Our Services

  • Analyze usage patterns to improve features and user experience
  • Develop new features based on user needs
  • Conduct beta testing and quality assurance
  • Fix bugs and technical issues
  • Perform internal research and development

3.3 To Communicate With You

  • Send service announcements and updates
  • Respond to your support requests and inquiries
  • Send security alerts and important notices
  • Request feedback about our Services
  • Send marketing communications (you can opt-out at any time)

3.4 For Security and Legal Compliance

  • Protect against fraud, abuse, and security threats
  • Verify user identity and prevent unauthorized access
  • Comply with legal obligations and regulations
  • Enforce our Terms of Service and other policies
  • Respond to legal requests and prevent harm

3.5 With Your Consent

  • Process information for any other purpose disclosed to you at collection
  • Use data for purposes you specifically authorize

We Do NOT:

  • Sell your personal information or financial data to anyone
  • Use your budget or transaction data for advertising
  • Share your financial information with advertisers
  • Access your budget data unless you request support

4. Third-Party Services and Data Sharing

4.1 Financial Data Aggregation Services

We partner with third-party financial data aggregation services to securely connect your financial accounts. We currently use Plaid Inc. as our financial data provider.

When you use our bank connection feature:

  • You authorize our financial data provider to access your financial account information on our behalf
  • Your bank credentials are transmitted directly to the provider using encryption
  • Sakura receives only the financial data necessary to provide our budgeting services
  • The provider's use of your information is governed by their own privacy policy
  • You can revoke access at any time through the provider's portal or within Sakura Budget settings

Current Provider Information:

  • Provider: Plaid Inc.
  • Privacy Policy: Plaid End User Privacy Policy
  • Manage Access: Plaid Portal

Important Security Note: We NEVER see or store your bank login credentials (username and password). These credentials are handled exclusively by our financial data provider using bank-level encryption. Sakura cannot access your bank login information at any time.

4.2 Service Providers

We share information with trusted service providers who assist in operating our Services:

  • Cloud Infrastructure: Secure data storage and hosting services
  • Payment Processors: Subscription billing and payment processing
  • Email Services: Transactional and marketing email delivery
  • Analytics Providers: Usage analytics and performance monitoring
  • Security Services: Fraud detection and prevention
  • Customer Support: Help desk and support ticket management

These service providers are contractually obligated to protect your information and use it only for the specific services they provide to us.

4.3 Business Transfers

If Sakura is involved in a merger, acquisition, sale of assets, or bankruptcy, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will notify you via email and/or prominent notice on our website before your information becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

4.4 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information when required by law or to:

  • Comply with legal process, court orders, or government requests
  • Enforce our Terms of Service and other agreements
  • Protect our rights, property, or safety, or that of our users or others
  • Investigate fraud, security issues, or technical problems
  • Prevent harm or illegal activity

4.5 Aggregated and De-Identified Data

We may share aggregated or de-identified information that cannot reasonably be used to identify you. For example, we may share statistics about platform usage or industry trends, but these will never include your personal financial data.

5. Data Security and Protection

We implement industry-leading security measures to protect your information:

5.1 Encryption and Security Standards

  • 256-bit AES Encryption: All data is encrypted at rest using bank-level encryption
  • TLS/SSL Encryption: All data transmission uses secure HTTPS connections
  • PCI-DSS Compliance: Payment data handling meets Payment Card Industry standards
  • Secure Authentication: Password hashing using industry-standard algorithms
  • Multi-factor Options: Additional security layers for account access

5.2 Operational Security

  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Continuous monitoring for security threats and vulnerabilities
  • Restricted access controls with role-based permissions
  • Secure development practices and code reviews
  • Employee training on data protection and security
  • Incident response and breach notification procedures

5.3 Your Security Responsibilities

You play an important role in protecting your account:

  • Choose a strong, unique password
  • Never share your account credentials
  • Log out after using shared or public devices
  • Keep your email account secure
  • Report suspicious activity immediately

5.4 Security Limitations

While we implement robust security measures, no system is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your information. In the event of a data breach affecting your personal information, we will notify you as required by applicable law.

6. Data Retention and Deletion

6.1 How Long We Keep Your Information

We retain your information for as long as necessary to provide our Services and fulfill the purposes described in this Privacy Policy. Specific retention periods include:

  • Active Account Data: Retained while your account is active
  • Financial Transaction Data: Retained for up to 7 years to comply with financial record-keeping requirements
  • Support Communications: Retained for 3 years for quality assurance and legal compliance
  • Usage Logs: Retained for 1 year for security and analytics purposes
  • Marketing Data: Retained until you opt-out or request deletion

6.2 Account Deletion

When you delete your account:

  • Your account access is immediately terminated
  • Your personal information and budget data are queued for deletion
  • Complete deletion from all systems, including backups, occurs within 60 days
  • Some information may be retained if required by law or for legitimate business purposes (e.g., fraud prevention, resolving disputes)

6.3 Data Export Before Deletion

Before deleting your account, we recommend exporting your data using our data export tools. After deletion is complete, we cannot recover your information.

7.1 Access and Correction

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Update or correct inaccurate information
  • Review your data processing activities

You can access and update most of your information directly through your account settings.

7.4 Account Deletion

You can delete your account at any time through:

8. California Privacy Rights (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA).

8.1 Categories of Information We Collect

In the past 12 months, we have collected the following categories of personal information:

  • Identifiers: Email address, account username, IP address
  • Financial Information: Bank account information, transaction data, budget allocations (through third-party financial data providers)
  • Commercial Information: Subscription and payment history
  • Internet Activity: Browsing history on our platform, usage patterns
  • Geolocation Data: General location from IP address (not precise location)

8.2 Your California Privacy Rights

California residents have the right to:

Right to Know:

  • Request disclosure of personal information we collect, use, and share
  • Request specific pieces of personal information we have collected
  • Know the categories of sources from which we collected information
  • Know our business purposes for collecting or selling information

Right to Delete:

  • Request deletion of personal information we have collected
  • Receive confirmation when deletion is complete (within 60 days)

Right to Correct:

  • Request correction of inaccurate personal information

Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information:

  • Limit our use and disclosure of sensitive personal information (including financial account information)
  • We only use your financial information to provide our budgeting services

Right to Opt-Out:

  • We do NOT sell your personal information
  • We do NOT share your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising

Right to Non-Discrimination:

  • We will not discriminate against you for exercising your privacy rights
  • We will not deny service, charge different prices, or provide different quality of service

8.3 How to Exercise Your California Rights

To exercise any of these rights, you may:

  • Email us at privacy@sakurabudget.com with "California Privacy Request" in the subject
  • Use the data request form in your account settings
  • Call our privacy hotline (contact information provided upon request)

8.4 Verification Process

To protect your privacy, we must verify your identity before fulfilling requests. We may ask you to:

  • Confirm your email address and account details
  • Provide additional identifying information
  • Answer security questions

You may designate an authorized agent to make requests on your behalf. The agent must provide written authorization or a power of attorney.

8.5 Response Timing

We will respond to verified requests within 45 days. If we need more time (up to 90 days total), we will notify you of the extension and the reason.

8.6 California "Shine the Light" Law

California Civil Code Section 1798.83 permits California residents to request certain information about disclosure of personal information to third parties for direct marketing. We do not share personal information with third parties for their direct marketing purposes.

9. GLBA Privacy Notice (Financial Privacy)

The Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA) requires us to tell you how we collect, share, and protect your personal financial information.

9.1 What Information Do We Collect?

We collect nonpublic personal information about you from:

  • Information you provide on applications and forms (email, name)
  • Information about your transactions with us (subscription payments, service usage)
  • Information from third-party financial data providers (financial account and transaction data)

9.2 What Information Can We Disclose?

We may disclose the following information to companies that perform services on our behalf:

  • Information you provide (email, account preferences)
  • Information about your transactions (budget data, transaction categorizations)
  • Information from third-party providers (account balances, transaction details)

9.3 How We Protect Your Information

To protect your personal information from unauthorized access and use, we implement security measures including:

  • Computer safeguards and secured files and buildings
  • 256-bit encryption and secure socket layer (SSL) technology
  • Restrictions on employee access to personal information
  • Employee confidentiality agreements and training

9.4 Your Privacy Choices

You can limit certain sharing:

  • Contact us at privacy@sakurabudget.com to limit our sharing with affiliates for marketing purposes
  • We do not share your information with non-affiliates for marketing purposes, so there is no opt-out for this type of sharing

9.5 Former Customers

If you close your account or become an inactive customer, we will continue to adhere to the privacy practices described in this notice.

10. Children's Privacy

Sakura Budget is not intended for children under 18 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 18. If we learn we have collected information from a child under 18, we will delete that information as quickly as possible.

If you believe we have collected information from a child under 18, please contact us immediately at privacy@sakurabudget.com.

11. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

11.1 Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies (Required):

  • Session management and authentication
  • Security and fraud prevention
  • Load balancing and performance

These cookies are necessary for the platform to function and cannot be disabled.

Analytics Cookies (Optional):

  • Usage statistics and performance metrics
  • Feature usage and engagement tracking
  • Error tracking and debugging

You can disable these through your cookie preferences.

Marketing Cookies (Optional):

  • Marketing campaign effectiveness
  • Referral source tracking
  • Advertising attribution

You can opt-out through cookie preferences or browser settings.

11.2 Third-Party Analytics

We use third-party analytics services that use cookies and similar technologies:

  • Google Analytics (with IP anonymization enabled)
  • These services collect data about your use of our platform
  • Data is used in aggregate form only

11.3 Managing Cookie Preferences

You can control cookies through:

  • Our cookie preference center (accessible in account settings)
  • Browser settings (most browsers allow you to refuse cookies)
  • Industry opt-out tools like NAI Opt-Out or DAA Opt-Out

11.4 Do Not Track

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature. Our platform does not currently respond to DNT signals, but we provide opt-out options as described above.

If you access our Services from outside the United States, please be aware that your information will be transferred to, stored, and processed in the United States where our servers are located and we operate. The data protection laws in the United States may differ from those in your country.

By using our Services, you consent to the transfer of your information to the United States and its processing as described in this Privacy Policy.
